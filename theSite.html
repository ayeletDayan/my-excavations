The Site

The site is located on a low hill where remains of an extensive settlement in which kurkar (sandstone) building stones were found, along with the remains of installations and sherds dating from the Roman to the Ottoman periods. At Horbat Meshullam, c. 1.5 km southwest of the excavation area, remains were excavated in the past of an installation dating to the Roman-Byzantine period. In a survey that preceded the excavation at Gan Ha-Darom, remains of architecture: kurkar stones, some dressed, were found protruding on the surface, perhaps the remains of a structure, as well as numerous sherds, dated mostly to the Byzantine period. These included fragments of a tabun, jars, kraters, a casserole and a few sherds, dating from the late Roman-early Byzantine periods.
